# Pillar 11: Attraction (Directional Pull)

## 📂 1. Philosophy: The Gradient of Desire
* [cite_start]**Core Logic**: Attraction is the tendency to move toward certain states or entities[cite: 49, 69]. [cite_start]It is a directional pull that guides behavior without the application of brute energy[cite: 49].
* [cite_start]**Guidance without Force**: Unlike **Force**, which enacts change directly, Attraction creates the movement gradients that define "internal goals"[cite: 38, 49].
* [cite_start]**The Inverse of Repulsion**: Repulsion is mathematically treated as the inverse of Attraction; therefore, no separate pillar is required for "push" mechanics[cite: 49, 69].
* [cite_start]**Incentive Alignment**: In social and corporate units, Attraction represents the incentives, desires, and demand that pull units toward clustering or resource acquisition[cite: 49, 69].

## 📂 2. Economic: The Driver of Investment
[cite_start]In the Net Value Equation ($V_{net}$), Attraction is the primary driver of **Investment (I)**[cite: 4, 75, 80].

* [cite_start]**Resource Flow**: Attraction determines the direction of resource allocation within the system[cite: 4, 27].
* [cite_start]**Market Demand**: In economic simulations, high Attraction in a specific unit naturally pulls Investment and capital toward it[cite: 5, 27].
* [cite_start]**Growth Condition**: Systemic growth is triggered when Attraction, **Cohesion**, and **Relation** exceed specific thresholds[cite: 80, 84].

## 📂 3. Logic: Functional Mechanics
*Based on `pillars_api.cpp` and `pillars_api.py` logic:*
* [cite_start]**Movement Vector**: The velocity of an entity is adjusted by the gradient of the Attraction field: `e.velocity += gradient * e.attraction`[cite: 74, 84].
* [cite_start]**Clustering**: When paired with Cohesion, Attraction leads to colony formation and the growth of organizational "clusters"[cite: 74, 84].
* [cite_start]**Trajectory Control**: It guides behavior by creating pull toward goals or other entities without direct forceful interaction[cite: 49, 69].

## 📂 4. Interaction: How Attraction Touches Others
| Interaction | Result |
