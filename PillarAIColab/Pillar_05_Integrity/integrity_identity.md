# Pillar 05: Integrity (Coherence of Identity)

## 📂 1. Philosophy: Consistency of Self
* [cite_start]**Core Logic**: Integrity is the consistency of a system with itself across time and stress.
* [cite_start]**The Identity Anchor**: It answers the foundational question: *Does this system remain itself?* [cite: 42]
* [cite_start]**Alignment**: It functions to align internal components, preventing fragmentation and maintaining a recognizable identity even when subjected to Force or Harm[cite: 43].
* [cite_start]**Stability**: Examples include a structure that does not collapse under load or a person acting consistently with their values[cite: 43].

## 📂 2. Economic: The Weight of Reliability
[cite_start]In the Net Value Equation ($V_{net} = \frac{P \cdot S \cdot W}{C} - E - I - R - B - \lambda H$), Integrity is a primary driver of **Weight (W)**[cite: 4, 75].

* [cite_start]**Systemic Dependency**: High Integrity increases an entity's Weight ($W$), as the system can rely on its stable, consistent contribution[cite: 5, 6].
* [cite_start]**Reliability Factor**: Integrity acts as a "System Removal Test" multiplier; a unit with high integrity is harder to replace because its output is predictable and consistent[cite: 6, 78].
* [cite_start]**Production Quality**: Production ($P_e$) depends on coherence; without Integrity, execution (Force) becomes scattered and inefficient[cite: 77].

## 📂 3. Logic: Functional Mechanics
*Based on `pillars_api.cpp` and `pillars_api.py` logic:*
* [cite_start]**Identity Restoration**: Integrity acts as a restorative force, pulling a unit’s state back toward its "baseline" to prevent drift or corruption[cite: 72, 83].
* [cite_start]**Harm Mitigation**: It serves as a defensive layer; damage is calculated as `incoming_damage - Resistance - Integrity`[cite: 74, 78].
* [cite_start]**Constraint Satisfaction**: Governance decisions are validated by ensuring the new state vector respects the Integrity constraints of the unit[cite: 4, 79].
