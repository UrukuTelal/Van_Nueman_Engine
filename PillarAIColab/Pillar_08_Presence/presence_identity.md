# Pillar 08: Presence (Priority / Visibility)

## 📂 1. Philosophy: Detectable Existence
* [cite_start]**Core Logic**: Presence is the degree to which an entity is perceived and impactful in a system[cite: 46, 66].
* [cite_start]**Priority Weighting**: It determines the visibility and priority of a unit, acting as the "loudness" of its signal within the environment[cite: 46, 66].
* [cite_start]**Impact Radius**: High Presence ensures that an entity's actions and states are not just occurring, but are being accounted for by the Awareness of others[cite: 46, 66].
* [cite_start]**Revealing Force**: It acts as the force that reveals the entity's state to the rest of the system[cite: 34, 54].

## 📂 2. Economic: The Driver of Scale and Visibility
[cite_start]In the Net Value Equation ($V_{net} = \frac{P \cdot S \cdot W}{C} - E - I - R - B - \lambda H$), Presence is a key controller of **Scale (S)**[cite: 4].

* [cite_start]**Operational Reach**: Presence determines the effective visibility of a unit's scale[cite: 4].
* [cite_start]**Attention Allocation**: It governs the "Priority / Visibility" operational function, managing how the system allocates attention and resources[cite: 1, 20].
* **Sublinear Scaling**: Larger units rely on Presence to maintain influence as they scale, though they typically scale sublinearly.

## 📂 3. Logic: Functional Mechanics
*Based on `pillars_api.cpp` and `pillars_api.py` logic:*
* [cite_start]**Visibility Calculation**: Presence determines who gets noticed; an observer's weighting of a neighbor is often proportional to the neighbor's Presence divided by distance[cite: 73, 84].
* [cite_start]**Response Weighting**: High Presence entities trigger more frequent or intense responses from the system[cite: 46, 66].
* [cite_start]**Normalization**: To prevent domination, Presence must often be normalized by weighting influence against the Awareness (data accuracy) of the system[cite: 8].
