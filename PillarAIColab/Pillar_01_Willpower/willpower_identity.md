# Pillar 01: Willpower (Persistence of Intent)

## 📂 1. Philosophy: Continuity of Direction
* [cite_start]**Core Logic**: Willpower is the ability to maintain direction across time despite resistance[cite: 38, 58].
* [cite_start]**Intent vs. Desire**: It is not desire (**Attraction**) or action (**Force**); it is the persistence of intent[cite: 38, 58].
* [cite_start]**Stability against Noise**: It stabilizes behavior against environmental noise, local disturbances, and internal distractions[cite: 59].
* [cite_start]**Temporal Consistency**: Willpower converts a momentary intention into a long-term, consistent trajectory[cite: 59].

## 📂 2. Economic: The Driver of Investment
[cite_start]In the Net Value Equation ($V_{net} = \frac{P \cdot S \cdot W}{C} - E - I - R - B - \lambda H$), Willpower is a primary driver of **Investment (I)**[cite: 4, 75].

* [cite_start]**Strategic Persistence**: It governs the "Strategic Persistence" operational function, ensuring long-term direction[cite: 2, 20].
* **Production Persistence**: Production ($P_e$) depends on Willpower to ensure that Force is applied consistently over time rather than in sporadic bursts.
* [cite_start]**Resource Allocation**: Along with **Attraction** and **Memory**, Willpower drives the allocation of resources toward sustained goals[cite: 4, 27].

## 📂 3. Logic: Functional Mechanics
*Based on `pillars_api.cpp` and `pillars_api.py` logic:*
* [cite_start]**Trajectory Maintenance**: Entities use Willpower to maintain their desired direction: `e.velocity += desired_direction * e.willpower`[cite: 72].
* **Drift Control**: It is used to pull pillar states toward a baseline, overriding local fluctuations or "The Twist" of **Distortion**.
* [cite_start]**Internal Evaluation**: In the governance loop, Willpower determines the "Strategic Persistence" during the `evaluate_constraints` phase[cite: 6, 24].

## 📂 4. Interaction: How Willpower Touches Others
| Interaction | Result |
