# Pillar 04: Resistance (Constraint & Entropy)

## 📂 1. Philosophy: The Stabilizing Opposition
* [cite_start]**Core Logic**: Resistance is the opposition to change, encompassing both stabilizing inertia and degrading entropy[cite: 41, 61].
* [cite_start]**Structure via Limitation**: It provides necessary structure by limiting chaos and slowing or blocking movement and transformation[cite: 41, 61].
* [cite_start]**Dual Nature**: While it preserves existing structures (e.g., friction, bureaucracy), it can also prevent necessary evolution[cite: 41, 61].
* [cite_start]**Stability Factor**: Resistance acts as a foundational stabilizer against incoming damage or disruptive forces[cite: 41, 61].

## 📂 2. Economic: The Controller of Friction
[cite_start]In the Net Value Equation ($V_{net} = \frac{P \cdot S \cdot W}{C} - E - I - R - B - \lambda H$), Resistance acts as a primary term for friction and a penalty for excess[cite: 4, 78].

* [cite_start]**Risk and Compliance**: It governs the "Risk / Compliance" operational function, acting as the stabilizing inertia of the unit[cite: 2, 20].
* [cite_start]**Buffer and Resilience**: Resistance drives the **Buffer (B)** term, providing the survival capacity under stress[cite: 4, 5].
* [cite_start]**Weight Penalty**: Excess resistance is penalized in the Pillar-Adjusted Weight ($W^*$) formula, as it can create artificial bottlenecks[cite: 5, 78].

## 📂 3. Logic: Functional Mechanics
*Based on `pillars_api.cpp` and `pillars_api.py` logic:*
* [cite_start]**Velocity Damping**: Resistance acts as friction, damping movement: `e.velocity *= (1 - local_resistance)`[cite: 72, 84].
* **Harm Mitigation**: It serves as a primary defense against disruption: `damage = local_harm - Resistance - Integrity`.
* [cite_start]**Constraint Validation**: Decisions are only valid if they maintain Resistance within an acceptable range to prevent either total stagnation or total chaos[cite: 4, 23].

## 📂 4. Interaction: How Resistance Touches Others
| Interaction | Result |
