# Pillar 03: Influence (Distributed Impact)

## 📂 1. Philosophy: The Capacity to Spread
* [cite_start]**Core Logic**: Influence is the capacity to alter other systems without direct forceful interaction.
* [cite_start]**Direct vs. Propagated**: While **Force** is direct, Influence is propagated through communication and signaling.
* [cite_start]**Reach Expansion**: It amplifies a unit's reach beyond its physical or localized limits by modifying the environment or neighbor states.
* [cite_start]**Cultural Signaling**: In social or corporate contexts, Influence represents communication and cultural signaling[cite: 20, 34].

## 📂 2. Economic: The Multiplier of Profit
[cite_start]In the Net Value Equation ($V_{net} = \frac{P \cdot S \cdot W}{C} - E - I - R - B - \lambda H$), Influence is a critical driver of **Profit (P)** and network amplification[cite: 4, 27, 75].

* [cite_start]**Network Leverage**: Influence (along with **Relation**) amplifies production output by leveraging dependencies and signaling effects[cite: 4, 77].
* [cite_start]**Market Presence**: It governs the "Communication / Culture" operational function, determining how a unit's value is perceived externally[cite: 20].
* [cite_start]**Network Dominance**: High Influence paired with high **Relation** leads to platform-like behavior and network dominance[cite: 9, 28].

## 📂 3. Logic: Functional Mechanics
*Based on `pillars_api.cpp` and `pillars_api.py` logic:*
* [cite_start]**State Modification**: Each unit modifies its neighbors' internal states based on its Influence value and a proximity-based kernel[cite: 72].
* [cite_start]**Broadcast Effect**: It acts like a broadcast field, where an entity's internal state is "pushed" onto nearby entities[cite: 72, 84].
* [cite_start]**Distortion Coupling**: High **Distortion** can "twist" Influence, turning propagated signals into propaganda or misinformation[cite: 13, 80].
