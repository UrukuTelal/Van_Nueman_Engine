# Pillar 15: Depth (The Latent Capacity)

## 📂 1. Philosophy: Stored Potential
* **Core Logic**: Depth is the stored pressure or unrealized possibility held within the system’s constraints.
* **Functional Role**: It represents the agent’s battery or spring; the amount of work the identity could perform if constraints were lifted.
* **Durability**: High Depth enables the agent to handle sudden spikes in complexity or "Black Swan" events.

## 📂 2. Economic: The Modulus of Resilience
* Depth acts as a denominator in Harm and Distortion equations, absorbing entropy and preventing integrity loss.
* **Ambition**: High Depth expands the scale of achievable goals and the length of multi-step planning horizons.
* **Resilience Reserve**: It allows the system to endure stress without immediate failure.

## 📂 3. Logic: Functional Mechanics
* **Capacity Buffer**: Depth stores latent capacity that can be drawn down as needed.
* **Ambition Scaling**: The available planning and problem-solving envelope is proportional to Depth.
* **Failure Absorption**: Depth increases the system’s ability to absorb Harm without fracturing Integrity.

## 📂 4. Interaction: How Depth Touches Others
| Interaction | Result |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Depth + Integrity** | **Durability**: Depth provides internal volume that prevents Integrity from cracking under high Resistance. |
| **Depth + Willpower** | **Ambition**: High Depth allows longer-term, multi-step planning and larger goal states. |
| **Depth + Flux** | **Expression**: Depth enables the system to express its latent capacity when throughput is available. |

---

## 🛠️ Folder-Level "Blackboard" Entry
**Primary Directive**: Preserve latent capacity and use it deliberately.
* **Management Fix**: If a task requires a wide context window but Depth is low, the validator should reject the delegation early to avoid wasted compute.
* **Warning**: Depth alone is not enough; it must be paired with Flux to express potential effectively.
